1 But Judas, who is also called Maccabaeus, and they that were with him, making their way privily into the villages, called to them their kinsfolk; and taking to them such as had continued in the Jews’ religion, gathered together as many as six thousand. 2 And they called upon the Lord, beseeching him to look upon the people that was oppressed by all; and to have compassion on the sanctuary also that had been profaned by the ungodly men; 3 and to have pity on the city also that was suffering ruin and ready to be made even even with the ground; and to hearken to the blood that cried to him; 4 and to remember also the lawless *Gr. destruction. slaughter of the innocent infants, and †Gr. concerning the blasphemies. the blasphemies that had been committed against his name; and to show his hatred of wickedness. 5 And when Maccabaeus had trained his men for service, the heathen at once found him irresistible, for that the wrath of the Lord was turned into pity. 6 ‡The Greek text of verses 6 and 7 is uncertain.
